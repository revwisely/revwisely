Sales can be a challenging job. It requires talking to customers, solving their problems, and keeping track of important details. But what if there were a way to make it easier? That’s where Generative AI (GenAI) comes in. This smart technology helps sales teams work faster and more efficiently.

Popular GenAI Tools for Sales

Many different AI tools help sales teams in various parts of their work. OpenAI's GPT, Google's Gemini, and Perplexity AI have popularized the technology. AI email platforms craft custom messages. They use customer data, past interactions, and industry trends. Call summarization tools use natural language processing (NLP) to turn conversations into text. They pick out key points and make summaries. CRM automation tools use AI to update records. They suggest the best next steps. They also provide insights on possible sales opportunities. Embedding generative AI in sales solutions helps businesses automate tasks. Better decision-making and more efficient sales processes are the result.

Here are 20 GenAI use cases for sales:

#1 Writing Personalized Emails

Reaching out to customers through email is a big part of sales. But writing each email from scratch takes time. AI email tools can draft messages tailored to each customer's interests and needs. Begin by entering basic customer info and preferences. This will create a relevant email draft for you.

#2 Summarizing Calls & Follow-Ups

After a sales call, it’s important to remember what was discussed. AI-driven tools can generate post-call summaries, highlighting key points and action items. It can also draft follow-up messages so salespeople don’t have to write them from scratch. This will help create summaries and follow-up emails automatically.

#3 Providing Live Call Support

During a sales call, a customer might ask a tough question or object. Real-time AI assistants can listen to calls. They provide helpful information like case studies, technical answers, and competitive comparisons. Start by linking real-time AI assistants to your call software and training them on company knowledge. You can listen to calls and get helpful info. This includes case studies, technical answers, and competitive comparisons.

#4 Keeping CRM Data Clean & Updated

Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems store important details about customers. However, updating and organizing this data can be time-consuming. AI-powered CRM systems can automatically fill in and tidy up records. Start by enabling AI-powered automation within your CRM system to sync and clean data regularly.

#5 Creating Competitive Battlecards

Salespeople need to know how their company compares to competitors. For example, AI tools can create and update battlecards. These are quick-reference guides that show key advantages and competitive differences. Start by entering competitor data and main product differences into AI tools to gather competitive intelligence and draft copy.

#6 Writing RFP Responses

When companies need vendors, they often ask for formal proposals (RFPs). Writing these can take a lot of time. AI tools can help by quickly drafting responses, and making sure they are professional and detailed. Start by feeding the AI previous RFPs and important company details to generate responses. (RFPs) when choosing a vendor.

#7 Drafting Proposals & Contracts

Creating business proposals and contracts is another area where GenAI shines. It can draft these documents based on templates and previous examples, saving sales teams time. Start by uploading standard templates and deal-specific details into the AI system.

#8 Speeding Up Legal Reviews

Contracts and proposals often require legal review. GenAI can help by identifying risks, redlining documents, and suggesting changes. Start by integrating AI with your contract management software for automatic risk detection.

#9 Researching Accounts & Market Trends

To sell effectively, sales teams need to understand their customers and industry trends. GenAI can quickly gather information about potential customers and analyze market shifts, helping salespeople stay informed. Start by setting up AI to monitor relevant industry sources and summarize insights.

#10 Finding Engagement Triggers

Knowing when to reach out to a potential customer is key. GenAI can track news articles, job postings, and industry updates to identify the best times to engage with prospects. Start by configuring AI to monitor specified news feeds and alert sales teams to engagement opportunities.

#11 Simulating Sales Conversations

Practice makes perfect. GenAI can serve as a virtual customer for sales role-play. This helps sales teams sharpen their skills and enhance their pitches. Start by inputting common customer objections and scenarios for AI-generated role-play exercises.

#12 Enabling Continuous Learning

Sales is always changing, and ongoing learning is important. GenAI can help you create tailored training materials. This helps salespeople keep up with new strategies and industry trends. Start by creating an AI-driven learning plan based on product information and sales performance data.

#13 Creating Custom Sales Materials

Each customer has different needs. GenAI creates tailored brochures, presentations, and sales materials. It matches these to specific customer interests. Give your AI tool of choice customer profiles and product details to create personalized materials.

#14 Analyzing Wins & Losses

Understanding why a deal was won or lost can help improve future sales strategies. GenAI can analyze sales data to identify patterns and provide insights into what worked and what didn’t. Start by linking AI to past sales data and setting up analysis parameters.

#15 Automating Prospecting & Lead Response

Finding new customers can be challenging. GenAI automates outbound prospecting. It finds potential leads and sends them personalized messages. It can also handle inbound inquiries, ensuring no potential customer is overlooked. Start by integrating AI with your CRM and marketing platforms to automate lead outreach.

#16 Running Product Demos

Product demonstrations help customers understand how a product works. GenAI can assist in running and personalizing these demos, making them more engaging and effective. Start by providing AI with demo scripts and interactive content for customized presentations.

#17 Scheduling Meetings

Coordinating meetings with customers and team members can take time. GenAI can handle scheduling tasks, ensuring meetings are set up efficiently. Start by connecting AI with your calendar and email system for automatic scheduling.

#18 Managing Initial Customer Interactions

When customers have questions, they often expect quick answers. AI chatbots and voice assistants can answer initial questions. They help customers find the right information or connect them with a sales rep. Start by deploying AI chatbots on your website and setting predefined responses.

#19 Generating Deal Review Questions

After a deal is closed, sales teams review the process to see what worked. GenAI can generate insightful questions for deal reviews, helping teams improve their strategies. Start by training AI to analyze past sales data and suggest relevant review questions.

#20 Creating Account Plans

Sales teams need structured plans for managing customer relationships. GenAI can help create these plans. This ensures a clear way to manage and grow customer accounts. Start by inputting key account details and business goals for AI-generated plans.

Setting Priorities for GenAI in Sales

Choosing the right places to use GenAI in sales starts with setting priorities. Business leaders should spot the biggest challenges in their sales process. This could be finding leads, following up with customers, or managing data. One simple way to prioritize is by looking at how much impact an AI solution will have compared to how easy it is to implement. Tasks that are both high-impact and easy to introduce, such as automating emails or summarizing calls, should be the first focus.

The Future of Sales with GenAI

Generative AI is revolutionizing sales, making processes faster, smarter, and more efficient. AI helps sales teams by automating repetitive tasks. It provides real-time insights and boosts customer engagement. This lets teams focus on what matters: building relationships and closing deals. AI will play a bigger role in sales. This will open new chances to boost revenue and increase productivity. To succeed, use AI wisely. Focus on important use cases that match your business goals. Embracing GenAI today means staying ahead in the competitive world of sales tomorrow.
